Is Owning A Pet Worth It?

Owning a pet is so common that more often than not when you speak ask someone, they'll tell you that they've owned a pet at some point in their lives. Some people love their pets so much they consider them as family members. On that note, people go to great lengths to take care of their pets but rarely think of the ways their pets take care of them. For example, it's been said that people who own pets are usually in better health than those who don't. Is that really the case though? I've done some digging and below are my findings.

Pros Cons
Pets such as dogs improve one's physical health such as cholesterol and blood pressure due to their requiring daily walks which is good for the owner. Pets such as rodents and cats can be especially harmful to pregnant people due to diseases they can expose them to.
Pets improve one's mental health by giving their owners more circumstances to talk to other people. Owning a pet can expose you to allergens.
The responsibility that comes with having to take care of a pet causes their owner to keep up with a daily routine. Owning a pet can be expensive. Depending on the type of pet and its size, a pet owner can spend hundreds or thousands of dollars a year.
